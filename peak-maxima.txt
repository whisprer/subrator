The reason this curve looks the way it does is rooted in how numbers grow. Think of it like this:

To get from a leading digit of 1 to a leading digit of 2, a value must grow by 100% (e.g., from $100 to $200).

Because growth in the natural world (populations, wealth, radioactive decay) is usually exponential or percentage-based rather than simple addition, numbers spend significantly more "time" with a leading digit of 1 than they do with any other digit.
