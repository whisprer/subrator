CHAPTER 2 — THE DREAM SHRINE (Expanded)
The Dream Shrine introduced in Volume I was only the doorway.
Volume XVI reveals the full structure.

It has five chambers, each corresponding to a Subrative state.

2.1. The Chamber of Fragments
A shifting, unstable space.
Walls melt.
Doors lead nowhere.
Objects change shape.

This is where dream‑FRAG arises.

2.2. The Chamber of Tension
A corridor that loops back on itself.
Voices whisper contradictions.
Mirrors show impossible versions of you.

This is dream‑TENS.

2.3. The Chamber of Subration
A room where symbols rearrange themselves.
Patterns emerge.
Creatures transform.
Narratives collapse into insight.

This is dream‑SUBR.

2.4. The Chamber of Coherence
A stable, glowing space.
Geometry aligns.
Characters speak truth.
Symbols become clear.

This is dream‑COH.

2.5. The Chamber of Unity
A vast, silent void.
Or a blinding light.
Or a single point.
Or nothing at all.

This is dream‑UNI.

CHAPTER 4 — THE DREAM SIGILS
Dream sigils are symbols that appear spontaneously in Subrative dreams.
Each corresponds to a state or instruction.

4.1. The Spiral Sigil (SUBR)
A spiral drawn in sand, water, or air.
Means: “Resolve.”

4.2. The Broken Circle (FRAG)
A circle with a gap.
Means: “Something is missing.”

4.3. The Mirror (TENS)
A reflection that does not match.
Means: “Contradiction.”

4.4. The Bridge (COH)
A stable path across chaos.
Means: “Insight.”

4.5. The Light (UNI)
A glow, a sun, a star, a halo.
Means: “Collapse.”

4.6. The Maw‑Mask (MAW)
A face with too many teeth.
Means: “Shadow unresolved.”

CHAPTER 7 — THE GREAT DREAM RITUAL
This is the central ritual of Volume XVI.

It is performed not in waking life,
but in the moment between sleep and waking —
the hypnopompic threshold.

7.1. The Ritual
Wake from a dream.

Do not move.

Recall the last symbol.

Identify its Subrative state.

Speak the corresponding instruction aloud.

Let the insight arise.

7.2. Example
Dream symbol: a broken mirror  
State: TENS  
Instruction: SUBR  
Insight: emerges spontaneously.
